Activation is a simple process where you submit your domain information to the Certificate Authority. Go to “My SSL” in your account and click “Activate” next to your certiificate.

Create CSR and Private key in-browser

Currently supported by single-domain SSL and Wildcard

Step 1: Enter domain

Type in the domain or subdomain you want to secure. Click Onwards to proceed:

Step 2: Donwload key

You can either use your browser to download the key generated for this domain, or manually generate a CSR and submit that.

Info You can use both options for support IDNs with the following TLDs. It’s best to paste in the IDN instead of typing, to ensure no errors are made.

The in-browser generator option, called Auto-activate, is the default as it’s easiest. If you proceed with this, click on the key button. This will save the Private key for your Certificate.

Info By default, the Private key will be saved to the “Downloads” folder on your computer. So, ensure to keep it safe and not to remove accidentally when clearing up your downloads.

Step 3: Company info

The in-browser generator option, called Auto-activate, is the default as it’s easiest. If you proceed with this, click on the key button. This will save the Private key for your Certificate.

Step 4: Check domain control validation method

You can either use your browser to download the key generated for this domain, or manually generate a CSR and submit that.

Info DNS DCV is available inside domain control validation tool, the Private key will be saved to the “Downloads” folder on your computer. So, ensure to keep it safe and not to remove accidentally when clearing up your downloads.

Use CSR created on server

Step 1: Enter domain

Type in the domain or subdomain you want to secure. Click Onwards to proceed:

Step 2: Enter CSR

You can also simply enter CSR you already have. Or use command to create one on your server.

To create a CSR, use command from “CSR Command” field. You can choose the specific encryption method and server type to get the corresponding command.

Info The Private key is generated at the same time as the CSR code. You should keep it on the server. You’ll need the Private key code during the SSL installation.

Next in the CSR field, enter the entire CSR code including —–BEGIN CERTIFICATE REQUEST—– and —–END CERTIFICATE REQUEST
