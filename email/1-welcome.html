    2. Get started in three steps

    To use an SSL you first need to activate it and then install it on your server. Activation consists of two steps for a basic SSL and three steps for an SSL that requires organization validation:

    1. Submit your data to CA. CA is short for Certificate Authority — these are the guys who issue SSL certificates.
    2. Confirm that you control the domain you wish to secure
    3. Pass company checks: organization validation for an OV SSL or extended validatio for an EV SSL.

    Some jargon you need to know:

    • Your activation request is submitted to the CA in the form a CSR code.
    • During the activation process, a Private Key is created at the same time as the CSR. Guard it with your life!! (Or just save it somewhere you’ll remember — you’ll need it later to install SSL).

    3. Two ways to activate SSL

    We have implemented an automatic way to create the CSR and Private Key in your browser as an alternative to generating them yourself on the server. Please note that Windows servers require you to convert your SSL to PFX if the Private Key is not generated on the server.

    The Private Key will be saved to the “Downloads” folder on your computer by default with the name "site_com_key.zip".

    Automatic CSR and Private Key generation is not available for multi-domain certificates, so you will have to generate your CSR and Private Key using the method outlined below.

    The more traditional way to generate a CSR and Private Key is on your hosting server. Here you can find the methods of how to do this on the most popular servers and control panels here.

    No matter how you choose to create your Private Key, if it’s lost or stolen, you will need to get your SSL reissued.

    4. The 100 million ways to install SSL

    Once the CA issues your SSL, you’ll get an email from them with the SSL file and CA bundle. Alternatively, you can download all the files from your SSL dashboard.

    In case you chose to create CSR and Private Key automatically in browser, take the SSL file, CA bundle and the Private Key file you saved to your “Downloads” folder earlier and upload them to your server.

    If you generated your own CSR on the server, just upload the SSL File and CA bundle — your Private Key is already there.

    5. SSL renewal: time to start over

    When it’s time to renew, you’ll need to go through the whole process again, ugh. It might seem like a pain, but it’s for your benefit. Honest. To minimize the chance of someone impersonating your site or stealing your private key, as well as ensuring that a company that received an SSL still exists, the lifespan of an SSL is limited.

    So, when your SSL dies, your CA gives you a new one for the same domain. Before this happens, you will get an email from us alerting you that the time to renew is coming soon.

    You can also purchase a brand new cert from SSLs.com store and activate it for the same domain, but the benefit of buying from the renewal email is that the remaining days of your current SSL will be transferred to your new one. The earliest time to renew is 30 days before expiration.

    What does the renewal process look like, anyway? Familiar, probably… Count to 10, rinse and repeat:

    1. Submit your data to CA
    2. Confirm that you control the domain you wish to secure.
    3. Pass company checks: organization validation for an OV SSL or extended validation for an EV SSL
