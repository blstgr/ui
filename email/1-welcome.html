    1. Your SSL dashboard

    First thing first. All your SSL are here. Here you can do everything. Activte, download, veiew all SSL, find, sort, renew.

    2. Get started in literally 3 steps

    To use SSL you need to activate it and install it on your server. Activation consists of 2 steps for basic HTTPS SSL and 3 steps for SSL that require organisation validation

    1. Submit your data to CA
    2. Confirm that you control domain
    3. Pass company checks: organisation validation for OV SSL or extended validation for EV SSL

    CA talks code language. You submit activation request in the form of CSR code Together with CSR, private key is created.

    3. Two ways to activate SSL

    We have implemented an automatic way to create CSR and private key in browser, so you don’t need to go back and forth to your hosting company and us and worry about creating all these files.

    Private key will be saved to your “Downloads” folder with the name "site_com_key.zip". Don't loose it, or you will need to reisse SSL.

    You can also use traditional way, and use CSR if you are already created one on your hosting server. In such a case, your private key will be on the server.

    4. 100M ways to install SSL

    Once CA issues SSL for you, you’ll get an email from them with SSL file and CA bundle or you can download all files from your SSL list

    Take SSL file, CA bundle and PK that was saved in your “Downloads” folder or only SSL files and CA bundle if you came to us with your own CSR and upload those files to your server. There are very many varations of how you can do that, all of them are based on your sever type.

    5. SSL renewal: start all over

    When it’s time to renew, we’ll need to go thorugh the whole process again, ugh. In order to minimize the chance of someone hacking your site, stealing your private key and to make sure a company that received an SSL still exists, any SSL lives for a limited time.

    Thus, when your SSL dies, CA gives you a new one for the same domain. You can also purchase a brand new cert from SSLs.com store and activate it for the same domain, but the benefit of buying from renewal email is that the remaining days will be transferred to your new SSL. The earliest time to renew is 30 days before expiration. Count to 10 and repeat:

    1. Submit your data to CA
    2. Confirm that you control domain
    3. Pass company checks: organisation validation for OV SSL or extended validation for EV SSL
